# Bazaria Sovereign Protocol: High-Ticket Asset & Escrow Compliance Manual

## 1. Protocol Architecture & Core Role
Bazaria operates strictly as a digital listing venue, software protocol, and high-fidelity marketing network. Bazaria is not a licensed real estate brokerage, title company, traditional escrow agent, or automotive dealership. The platform facilitates decentralized data matching, automated escrow routing via software hooks, and global registry visibility. It does not take legal title to, ownership of, or physical responsibility for any physical assets listed on the registry.

## 2. High-Ticket Deposit & Monetization Matrix
To eliminate transactional drag on high-value asset classes (such as Real Estate, Cars, Trucks, and RVs), the platform bypasses standard commission models on the total sale price. Instead, it operates on an upfront Deposit-Based Software Fee framework:
* **The Reservation Deposit:** The winning bidder or instant-buyer commits a 5% Intent Deposit via the platform's payment infrastructure to instantly pause the registry listing.
* **Network Processing Surcharges:** All baseline financial processing fees are passed transparently to the buyer upfront as an external network surcharge, preserving 100% of the platform's core profit margin:
  * **Credit Cards:** ~2.9% + $0.30 network surcharge added to the checkout total.
  * **Cryptocurrency / Native Bitcoin:** 1.5% flat network surcharge added to the checkout total.
  * **ACH Bank Transfers:** 0.8% network surcharge, strictly capped at a maximum of $7.00.
* **The Bazaria Cut (Listing & Documentation Fee):** A non-refundable software facilitation fee equal to 6% of the base deposit value is programmatically sliced from the incoming transaction and routed immediately to the Bazaria corporate reserve.
* **The Forwarded Escrow Hold:** The remaining balance of the deposit is safely held within a segregated, non-custodial Stripe Connect sub-ledger. Upon legal validation, this capital is routed directly to the designated closing attorney, title agency, or verified regional Listing Agent.

## 3. Merchant Breach & Default Protocol (Seller Cancellations)
If a Merchant (Seller) cancels a finalized auction or digital contract to pursue an off-platform transaction, the protocol enforces an automated financial and systemic penalty to protect registry integrity:
* **10% Liquidated Damages Levy:** The escrow system automatically seizes a 10% penalty directly out of the held transaction deposit balance.
* **Capital Redistribution Split:** 
  * **90% of the Deposit:** Released and securely returned to the Winning Bidder's original payment routing method.
  * **50% of the Retained Penalty (5% of total deposit):** Kept by Bazaria to settle the non-refundable Listing, Data, and Documentation processing fees.
  * **50% of the Retained Penalty (5% of total deposit):** Disbursed directly to the Winning Bidder as an automated Contract Breach Credit to compensate for market displacement.
* **Registry Disciplinary Action:** The defaulting Merchant’s "Verified Status" is instantly suspended, a permanent Contract Breach Log is appended to their database metadata, and listing privileges are restricted for a 90-day probationary period.

## 4. Legal Compliance & Liability Boundaries
* **Liability Ceiling:** Under no circumstances shall Bazaria LLC, its parent entities, or its developers be held liable for compensatory, punitive, incidental, or consequential damages resulting from a failed transaction, physical asset defect, title dispute, or merchant breach.
* **Sole and Exclusive Remedy:** The winning bidder’s sole remedy in the event of a seller-side cancellation is the return of their eligible escrow balance and the programmatic distribution of their 50% share of the liquidated damages penalty.
* **Execution Boundary:** All final deeds, titles, vehicle handoffs, and final payouts must be executed independently through licensed third-party professionals (closing attorneys, title registries, or certified DMV channels).
